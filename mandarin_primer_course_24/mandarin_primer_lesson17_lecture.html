Mandarin Primer — Lesson 17

Talking About Indusco

Lesson 17 begins with a small everyday conflict about oversleeping, breakfast, and hurrying to a factory visit, then opens into a much larger topic: the Indusco movement, wartime industrial relocation, cooperative light industry, science, machinery, returned students, native materials, and improving ordinary people’s lives. It is an excellent lesson for showing students how spoken Mandarin can move from ordinary complaint to economic and social explanation.

This page keeps the book’s original romanization system so it stays aligned with the scanned lesson.

Learning goals

What students should learn

  • Follow the two-part structure of the lesson: rushing out in the morning, then discussing Indusco while walking.
  • Understand everyday hurry-up language: getting up, washing, brushing teeth, making tea, waiting, blaming, being late, and walking instead of riding.
  • Learn how the lesson explains what Indusco means and how the Light Industrial Cooperative Movement started in wartime China.
  • Recognize vocabulary for machines, workers, technicians, capital, trade, handicraft, scientific method, specialists, engineers, returned students, research, and native materials.
  • See how Mandarin handles cause-and-effect, “one effort, two results,” and explanation of social benefit.
  • Notice the shift from conversational irritation to organized civic-economic reasoning.
Teacher lens

Why this lesson matters

Lesson 17 teaches practical life and national reconstruction in one flow. The speakers start by worrying about time, breakfast, and transportation, and end by discussing industry, science, jobs, and living standards.

For high-school students, this is especially effective because it shows that language is not divided into “simple life talk” versus “serious knowledge talk.” One conversation can move naturally from both.

Two-hour lecture flow

Suggested lecture sequence

1
Morning rush hook
Start with the comic complaint: it is already half past seven and one person is still accused of lying in bed and snoring.
2
Time and routine
Teach rising, washing, brushing, tea-making, delay, blame, and whether there is still enough time.
3
Travel to the factory
Map the route, transport options, and appointment constraints: five li, before eight, country roads.
4
What is Indusco?
Explain the term, then introduce the Light Industrial Cooperative Movement and its wartime origin.
5
Why it matters
Teach how Indusco helps workers, produces useful goods, uses science, and raises living standards.
6
Research and problem-solving + quiz
End with engineers, returned students, native materials, cheap useful goods, and the final moment of realizing they may be lost.
How to teach it well

Effective teaching moves for beginners

The source’s “To the Student” guidance stresses utterances, echoing, and grammar-through-exercises. Lesson 17 works especially well when the teacher makes students feel the scene change: first a rushing morning, then a walking conversation about society and economics.
  1. Teach the first page like a mini skit: bed, snoring, washing, tea, hurrying out the door.
  2. Then make a visible transition on the board from “personal delay” to “public purpose.”
  3. Use a simple timeline for the wartime story: war begins → factories move inland → workers and technicians suffer → industries are rebuilt.
  4. Put “problem → solution → benefit” on the board as the main logic of the Indusco half.
  5. Have students sort vocabulary into everyday life words versus industrial-development words, then show how the lesson connects them.
Likely beginner confusions

Where English speakers may stumble

Students may think the second half is abstract and difficult, but it becomes manageable once they see the repeated pattern: problem, movement inland, rebuilding, cooperation, scientific improvement, better life.
  • Final dou in chi-dean-bann le dou is an afterthought emphasis marker, not ordinary “all.”
  • .shanq appears where English would say “in,” as in being on the bed or on a surface.
  • le in enumeration can show “and what not” attitude rather than only past time or completion.
  • gongher does not literally mean “work together”; the notes explain that this popular interpretation conflicts with normal Chinese modifier order.
  • bujiannde means “not necessarily / not likely seen as,” and the notes say there is no ordinary positive *jiannde* form.
Dialogue walkthrough

Lesson 17 as a movement from hurry to explanation

This lesson becomes much easier when divided into two clear scenes.

Scene 1 Jeanjyr shiaw.huah! Chi-dean-bann le dou! Woo jiaw nii tzao.deal chiilai, nii jiow lao taang de chwang.shanq tzaynall daa-hu.lu.
This is ridiculous! It is already half past seven! I told you to get up early, and you just kept lying in bed snoring.
Scene 1 Woo i-dah-tzao jiow chii-chwang le ... Neme nii dan.wuh de shyrhowl tay duo le. Shii-lean le, shua-ya shuh-koou le, chuan i.shang, chuan wahtz, jih shyedall le ...
I got up as soon as it was morning ... Then you wasted too much time: washing your face, brushing your teeth, rinsing your mouth, putting on clothes and socks, tying your shoelaces ...
Scene 1 Nii feideei deeng shoei kai le, weyle yaw na kai-shoei chi-char chy dean.shin, yow deei deeng nemm banntian!
You insisted on waiting for the water to boil, and then for making tea with boiling water for breakfast we had to wait such a long time.
Scene 1 Bye guay jeyg guay neyg le. Jeh yeou sherm jeng.toul? Hairsh kuay tzoou b'ou!
Stop blaming this and blaming that. What is the point of arguing about it? Better hurry and go.
Scene 1 Jell naal lai yangche ia? Jell yee mei che tzuoh yee mei liu chyi yinqsh deei na jeau tzoou.
Where would rickshas come from here? There are no vehicles and no donkeys to ride; the hard fact is that we have to walk on our feet.
Scene 1 Jell li gongchaang yeou wuu-lii-duo dih. Tzarmen gen tamen iue de sh ba-dean chyan.ideal daw nall tsanguan ... Kuaydeal tzoou laidejyi.
The factory is more than five li from here. Our appointment is to get there a little before eight for the visit ... If we walk fast, we can still make it in time.
Scene 2 'Gongher' jiowsh 'Gongyeh Hertzuoh' heje 'Ching Gongyeh Hertzuoh Yunndonq' de jeandan shuo.faa.
“Gongher” is simply a shortened way of saying “industrial cooperatives” or “the light industrial cooperative movement.”
Scene 2 Jeyg yunndonq de lihshyy sh tsorng Mingwo ellshyrchi-nian chii-tourl de ... ney-tsyh de daa-janq a, bujiannde sh ig doan-shyrchyi de jannjeng.
The history of this movement begins in 1938 ... that war was not likely to be a short war.
Scene 2 Renren dou shiahle charngchyi diikanq de jyueshin, mooyii bae gehchuh gongchaang.lii de jichih a, yeou shiunnliann de gong.ren a, yeou jingyann de jihshy a ... ban-dawle neydih geh-sheeng.
Everyone was determined on long-term resistance, so machines, trained workers, and experienced technicians from the factories were moved into the inland provinces.
Scene 2 Tamen dou kuu de heen ... tsair bae gongyeh yow jiannsheh.chiilai. I-fangmiann ne, keeyii bangjuh neyshie mei shell gann mei fann chy de gong.ren ... torngshyr ne, yow keeyii tzaw.chu sheuduo ryhyonq bihshiu de wuhpiin lai.
They suffered a great deal before they finally rebuilt industry. On the one hand, they could help workers without jobs and food; at the same time, they could produce many daily necessities.
Scene 2 Re, tzemmyanql dawsh 'i-jeu leang-der,' ar? ... hao.chuh jiow tzay jell lo.
Ah, that is really “one effort, two results,” isn’t it? ... That is exactly where the advantage lies.
Scene 2 Tamen jyh.tzaw dongshi gen bann-shyh, chyuansh jawj keshyue fangfaa de. Farnsh keeyii yonq jichih de dihfangl jiow yonq jichih; keeyii bae jiow-fartz gaeliang de dihfangl, jiow gaecherngle shin-fartz.
In manufacturing and management, they proceed entirely according to scientific methods. Wherever machines can be used, they use them; where old methods can be improved, they change them into new methods.
Scene 2 I-fangmiann keeyii tzengjia shengchaan de lih.lianq, torngshyr ne, yow keeyii bae renmin de sheng.hwo cherng.duh geei tyi-gau le.
On the one hand, they can increase productive power, and at the same time they can raise the standard of living of the people.
Scene 2 Gongher.lii yeou sheuduo juanmen de rentsair ... lioushyuesheng ... Tamen i-tian-daw-woan yanjiow yonq tzeemvx jeandan-huah de jichih, yonq neeishie vx beendih de tsair.liaw, ranhow tzaw.de.chu shermvx yow yeou yonq yow may de pyan.yi de huoliwuh lai.
Within Indusco there are many specialists, including returned students from abroad. They keep researching simplified machinery and local materials in order to make useful goods that can also be sold cheaply.
Closing Tzarmen tzooule tzemm banntian, kee yeou iball luh l'a? ... koongpah tzoou-tsuoh le luh le ba. Diinghao wennx ren ba.
We have walked for such a long time — are we even halfway there yet? ... I am afraid we have taken the wrong road. We had better ask someone.

Core concepts

What the lesson teaches, concept by concept

1) Spoken complaint can carry rich grammar

afterthought dou
enumeration le
guay
  • The opening scene is full of natural spoken texture: emphatic afterthoughts, lists of actions, blame, and impatience.
  • This makes the everyday part of the lesson grammatically valuable, not just comic.

2) The lesson turns time pressure into motion

laidejyi
iue
tsanguan
  • The speakers calculate whether they can still arrive before eight for a factory visit.
  • This creates a clear action frame before the explanatory discussion begins.

3) Indusco is defined through abbreviation and correction

gongher
gongyeh hertzuoh
ching gongyeh hertzuoh yunndonq
  • The lesson explicitly teaches what the label “Indusco” stands for.
  • The notes also warn against the misleading folk translation “work together.”

4) Wartime relocation is the historical engine of the lesson

kanqjann
charngchyi diikanq
ban-daw neydih
  • The movement is explained as a response to long-term resistance during wartime.
  • Machines, workers, and technicians all move inland together.

5) Cooperation solves two problems at once

mei shell gann
ryhyonq bihshiu de wuhpiin
i-jeu leang-der
  • Indusco helps unemployed workers and produces useful daily necessities.
  • This is the core moral-economic point of the lesson.

6) Light industry is contrasted with trade and handicraft

tzybeen
mae.may
shoou.yih
  • The lesson asks why this is not just ordinary small business or handicraft.
  • The answer is scientific method, better organization, machinery, and systematic improvement.

7) Scientific method is framed as practical improvement

keshyue fangfaa
gaeliang
shin-fartz
  • Science here is not abstract theory. It means using machines where possible and improving old methods where they can be improved.
  • This helps students connect “science” to concrete production choices.

8) Expertise is socially useful

juanmen de rentsair
gongcherngshy
lioushyuesheng
beendih de tsair.liaw
  • The lesson emphasizes specialists, engineers, returned students, simplified machinery, and native materials.
  • The goal is not prestige but useful, affordable goods and a higher standard of living.

High-value classroom move

Use a two-scene performance

Divide the class into Scene 1 actors and Scene 2 explainers.
  1. One pair performs the oversleeping and rushing scene.
  2. Another pair continues the walk and explains Indusco to the class.
  3. The board records the shift from personal inconvenience to public purpose.

This helps students feel the lesson’s structure instead of treating it as a single block of text.

A key structural system

Problem → solution → benefit

Problem

mei shell gann, mei fann chy

Workers lose jobs and need livelihood.

Solution

gongyeh hertzuoh, yonq jichih, gaecherngle shin-fartz

Cooperation, machines, and improved methods.

Benefit

tzaw.chu ryhyonq bihshiu de wuhpiin, tyi-gau sheng.hwo cherng.duh

Useful goods and a higher standard of living.

Teaching payoff

This lesson gives students a clean template for explaining social programs in Mandarin.

Guided practice

Practice tasks for a two-hour lesson

Task A — Morning-rush retell

Students retell the first scene using sequence markers and routine verbs.

Task B — Why were they late?

Students identify each source of delay and explain which complaints are reasonable.

Task C — What is Indusco?

Students explain the term, when the movement started, and what problem it tried to solve.

Task D — Science and cheap useful goods

Students explain how engineers, research, simplified machines, and local materials fit together.

Quick understanding check

Two multiple-choice quiz questions

1) According to the notes, why is the popular translation of gongher as “work together” considered misleading?

Correct. The notes explicitly say that reading gongher as “work together” goes against the normal modifier-before-head pattern of Chinese grammar.
Not quite. The notes make a grammar point here, not just a dictionary point.

2) What is the clearest summary of the lesson’s social argument about Indusco?

Correct. The lesson explicitly frames Indusco as helping workers and producing useful goods, then extends that logic to improved production and living standards.
Not quite. The lesson’s key idea is social benefit through cooperative production, not just factories in the abstract.
Exit challenge

End-of-lecture spoken challenge

  1. Name two things that delayed the speakers in the morning.
  2. Explain what gongher stands for in the lesson.
  3. Describe, in English, why factories, workers, and technicians were moved inland.
  4. Give one example of how science changes production in the lesson.
  5. Explain the meaning of i-jeu leang-der in this context.
